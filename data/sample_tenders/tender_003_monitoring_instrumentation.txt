1. PROJECT BACKGROUND
National Highways is procuring specialist instrumentation and monitoring services for the A303 Stonehenge Improvement Scheme. The project involves construction of a 3.3km bored tunnel to remove the A303 from the World Heritage Site. This package covers real-time geotechnical and structural monitoring throughout the construction phase.

2. MONITORING SCOPE
2.1 Surface and Subsurface Monitoring
- 450 settlement monitoring points (surface)
- 85 inclinometers (subsurface movement)
- 120 vibrating wire piezometers (groundwater)
- 60 earth pressure cells (tunnel lining loads)
- 30 extensometers (heave/settlement at depth)

2.2 Structural Monitoring (Heritage Assets)
- Monitoring of 14 scheduled monuments within influence zone
- 6 crack monitoring arrays on existing structures
- Photogrammetric baseline survey and periodic repeat surveys
- Vibration monitoring at 8 receptor locations

2.3 Data Management and Reporting
- Automated data acquisition at 5-minute intervals from all instruments
- Real-time dashboard for project team access
- Trigger level alerting (Yellow/Amber/Red) with automated notifications
- Monthly instrumentation reports
- 5-year data retention and archive

3. TRIGGER LEVELS
Instrument Type           Green (Normal)    Amber (Alert)     Red (Action)
Surface settlement        < 5mm             5–15mm            > 15mm
Piezometric level         ± 0.5m            ± 0.5–1.5m        > ± 1.5m
Lining hoop stress        < 60% design      60–80% design     > 80% design
Vibration (PPV)           < 3 mm/s          3–8 mm/s          > 8 mm/s
