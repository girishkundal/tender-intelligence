INVITATION TO TENDER
Project: Northern Line Extension — Tunnel Boring Works
Contract Reference: NLE-TBW-2024-047
Issuing Authority: Transport for London (TfL)
Issue Date: 15 March 2024
Tender Return Date: 30 April 2024

1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
Transport for London invites tenders for the design, supply, and operation of tunnel boring machine (TBM) works for the Northern Line Extension Phase 2. The project involves the construction of approximately 3.2 kilometres of twin-bore running tunnels and one station box excavation in mixed ground conditions.

2. SCOPE OF WORKS
2.1 Tunnel Boring
- Twin-bore tunnel construction using Earth Pressure Balance (EPB) TBM
- Tunnel internal diameter: 5.6 metres
- Total tunnel length: 3,200 metres (two bores)
- Ground conditions: London Clay overlying Thanet Sand, with potential for mixed face conditions
- Maximum groundwater pressure: 2.5 bar

2.2 Tunnel Lining
- Precast concrete segmental lining, 275mm thickness
- Six segments plus key per ring
- Ring width: 1.4 metres
- Total rings: approximately 2,285

2.3 Instrumentation and Monitoring
- Real-time settlement monitoring using automated total stations
- Groundwater pressure monitoring via vibrating wire piezometers
- Structural monitoring of adjacent buildings within 50m of tunnel alignment
- Continuous data feed to project control room — minimum 15-minute intervals

3. GROUND CONDITIONS
Geotechnical Investigation Report (GIR) reference: NLE-GIR-2023-011 — available in data room.
Key parameters:
- Undrained shear strength (Cu): 80–150 kPa
- SPT N-values in Thanet Sand: 25–45
- Coefficient of earth pressure at rest (K0): 1.2–1.8
- Estimated settlement trough width parameter (i): 8–12 metres

4. PROGRAMME
Contract commencement: September 2024
TBM launch: November 2024
Tunnel breakthrough (first bore): August 2025
Tunnel breakthrough (second bore): February 2026
Practical completion: June 2026
Total contract duration: 22 months
